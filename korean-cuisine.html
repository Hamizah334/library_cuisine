Korean Cuisine Guide

Rice Dishes

Bibimbap
Mixed Rice Bowl

A colorful rice dish topped with assorted vegetables, sliced meat (usually beef), a fried egg, and gochujang (Korean chili paste). It’s mixed thoroughly before eating.

Kimchi Bokkeumbap
Kimchi Fried Rice

Spicy fried rice made with aged kimchi, rice, and sometimes diced pork or spam. Often topped with a fried egg.

Kimbap

Seaweed rice rolls filled with vegetables, egg, and meat or fish. Often compared to sushi rolls but with sesame oil-seasoned rice instead of vinegar.

Meat & Protein-Based Dishes

Bulgogi

Thinly sliced marinated beef grilled or stir-fried. Marinated with soy sauce, sugar, sesame oil, garlic, and pepper. Juicy and savory.

Samgyeopsal

Grilled pork belly typically cooked at the table. Served with dipping sauces, lettuce wraps, garlic, and kimchi.

Dakgalbi

Spicy stir-fried chicken with cabbage, rice cakes, and sweet potatoes. Cooked with gochujang and served sizzling hot.

Jeyuk Bokkeum

Spicy stir-fried pork made with gochujang, garlic, and onions. Bold in flavor and often eaten with rice.

Soups & Stews

Kimchi Jjigae

Hot and spicy stew made with fermented kimchi, tofu, pork, and vegetables. A staple comfort dish in Korean households.

Sundubu Jjigae

Soft tofu stew with vegetables, seafood or meat, and a spicy broth. Served bubbling hot in a stone bowl.

Doenjang Jjigae

Fermented soybean paste stew with tofu, vegetables, and sometimes clams. Earthy and savory in flavor.

Seolleongtang

Milky-white beef bone soup simmered for hours. Served with sliced beef and green onions, often seasoned at the table.

Vegetable & Side Dishes

Kimchi

Fermented cabbage or radish seasoned with chili, garlic, ginger, and fish sauce. Iconic Korean side dish rich in probiotics.

Namul

Seasoned vegetable side dishes, such as spinach, bean sprouts, or fernbrake, seasoned with sesame oil, garlic, and salt.

Japchae

Stir-fried glass noodles made from sweet potato starch, mixed with vegetables and sometimes meat. Savory and slightly sweet.

Pajeon

Savory pancake with green onions, often mixed with seafood or kimchi. Served with dipping soy sauce.

Snacks & Street Food

Tteokbokki

Chewy rice cakes cooked in a spicy and slightly sweet gochujang sauce. Often served with fish cakes and boiled eggs.

Hotteok

Sweet Korean pancakes filled with brown sugar, cinnamon, and chopped nuts. Crispy outside, gooey inside.

Odeng (Eomuk)

Fish cake skewers served in warm broth. Commonly found at street stalls during colder months.

Gimbap

Rice and fillings rolled in seaweed, cut into bite-sized slices. Great as an on-the-go snack or picnic food.

Desserts & Sweets

Bingsu

Shaved ice dessert topped with red beans, fruit, condensed milk, and rice cake pieces. Modern versions may include matcha, chocolate, or mango.

Yakgwa

Traditional honey cookies made from wheat flour, sesame oil, and ginger, then deep-fried and soaked in honey syrup.

Tteok (Rice Cakes)

Various chewy rice cakes, some sweet and others savory. Often used in celebrations and rituals.

Drinks

Sikhye

Sweet rice drink made from malt water. Often served cold as a dessert or after-meal refreshment.

Bori-cha

Roasted barley tea, caffeine-free, served hot or cold. Common daily drink in Korea.

Soju

Clear distilled spirit, often enjoyed during meals or social gatherings. Typically lower in alcohol than vodka, but varies by brand.
