Korean Cuisine Guide

Rice Dishes

Bibimbap
Mixed Rice Bowl

Bibimbap is a colorful and nutritious Korean rice dish topped with a variety of seasoned vegetables, sliced meat (usually beef), a fried egg, and gochujang (Korean chili paste). It is thoroughly mixed before eating to ensure every bite contains a harmonious blend of textures and flavors.

Kimchi Bokkeumbap
Kimchi Fried Rice

Kimchi Bokkeumbap is a spicy and savory fried rice dish made using well-fermented kimchi, leftover rice, and optional ingredients such as diced pork, Spam, or vegetables. It is often finished with a fried egg on top, adding creaminess and balance to the bold flavors.

Kimbap

Kimbap is a popular Korean snack made from seaweed-wrapped rice rolls filled with seasoned vegetables, egg, and meat or fish. It resembles Japanese sushi rolls, but the rice is flavored with sesame oil rather than vinegar, giving it a distinct Korean identity.

Meat & Protein-Based Dishes

Bulgogi

Bulgogi is a classic Korean dish made from thinly sliced beef marinated in a mixture of soy sauce, sugar, sesame oil, garlic, and pepper. The meat is then grilled or stir-fried until juicy and caramelized, delivering a savory and slightly sweet flavor loved by many.

Samgyeopsal

Samgyeopsal is a beloved Korean BBQ dish featuring thick slices of pork belly grilled at the table. Eaten with lettuce wraps, dipping sauces, garlic, and kimchi, it’s a hands-on meal that’s as social as it is delicious.

Dakgalbi

Dakgalbi is a spicy stir-fried chicken dish cooked with cabbage, chewy rice cakes (tteok), sweet potatoes, and gochujang. Often served sizzling at the table, it’s a hearty and fiery meal perfect for sharing.

Jeyuk Bokkeum

Jeyuk Bokkeum is a bold, spicy stir-fried pork dish marinated with gochujang, garlic, onions, and other spices. It’s full of umami and heat, often served with a side of steamed rice and fresh vegetables.

Soups & Stews

Kimchi Jjigae

Kimchi Jjigae is a spicy Korean stew made with fermented kimchi, pork or tuna, tofu, and various vegetables. Known for its warming and comforting qualities, it’s a staple in Korean households, especially during cold seasons.

Sundubu Jjigae

Sundubu Jjigae is a spicy soft tofu stew served bubbling hot in a stone bowl. It includes uncurdled tofu, vegetables, seafood or meat, and an egg cracked into the stew just before serving. It’s loved for its silky texture and bold flavors.

Vegetable & Side Dishes

Kimchi

Kimchi is Korea’s most iconic side dish made by fermenting cabbage or radish with chili pepper, garlic, ginger, and fish sauce. It is tangy, spicy, and full of probiotics that contribute to gut health.

Namul

Namul refers to an assortment of seasoned vegetables, typically including spinach, bean sprouts, fernbrake, and radish. These are lightly blanched and seasoned with sesame oil, garlic, soy sauce, or salt, served cold as side dishes.

Japchae

Japchae is a savory-sweet dish made from glass noodles (dangmyeon) stir-fried with a variety of vegetables, beef or egg, and seasoned with soy sauce and sesame oil. It’s often served on festive occasions in Korea.

Snacks & Street Food

Tteokbokki

Tteokbokki is a popular Korean street food made with chewy cylindrical rice cakes simmered in a bright red gochujang sauce. It’s sweet, spicy, and often includes fish cakes, scallions, and boiled eggs.

Hotteok

Hotteok are sweet Korean pancakes with a warm, gooey filling made from brown sugar, cinnamon, and crushed nuts. Crispy on the outside and soft inside, they are especially popular in winter markets.

Desserts & Sweets

Bingsu

Bingsu is a popular Korean shaved ice dessert topped with sweet toppings like red beans, fruits, condensed milk, and rice cake pieces. Modern variations include flavors like matcha, chocolate, and mango, offering a refreshing treat on hot days.

Yakgwa

Yakgwa is a traditional Korean sweet made from wheat flour, sesame oil, and honey syrup. These flower-shaped cookies are deep-fried and soaked in ginger honey syrup, making them fragrant, chewy, and rich in flavor.
