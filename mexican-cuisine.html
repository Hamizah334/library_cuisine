Mexican Cuisine Guide

Rice & Corn Dishes

Arroz Rojo
Mexican Red Rice

Classic side dish made with rice sautéed in oil and cooked with tomatoes, onions, garlic, and chicken broth. Often served alongside beans and meat dishes.

Tamales

Steamed corn dough (masa) filled with meats, cheese, or chilies, wrapped in corn husks. A traditional food for celebrations and holidays.

Elote

Grilled corn on the cob coated with mayonnaise, cheese (cotija), chili powder, and lime juice. A popular street food snack.

Meat & Protein-Based Dishes

Tacos al Pastor

Pork marinated in achiote and pineapple, cooked on a vertical spit. Served in corn tortillas with onions, cilantro, and salsa.

Carnitas

Slow-cooked pork that's tender on the inside and crispy on the outside. Commonly used in tacos, burritos, or tortas.

Carne Asada

Grilled and marinated beef, usually flank or skirt steak, sliced thin and served in tacos or as a main dish.

Chiles Rellenos

Large poblano peppers stuffed with cheese or meat, battered and fried, then served with tomato sauce.

Soups & Stews

Pozole

Traditional hominy soup made with pork or chicken, topped with lettuce, radish, onion, lime, and chili. Served during festivities.

Menudo

Spicy soup made with beef tripe, hominy, and chili. Often eaten on weekends or special occasions.

Caldo de Pollo

Mexican chicken soup with vegetables like carrots, potatoes, and zucchini. Served with rice and lime.

Vegetable & Bean Dishes

Nopales

Sliced cactus paddles cooked with tomatoes, onions, and chili. Served as a salad or side dish.

Frijoles Refritos

Refried beans cooked with lard or oil, garlic, and spices. Served as a side or filling for tacos and burritos.

Chiles en Escabeche

Pickled jalapeños, carrots, and onions in a vinegar-based marinade. Common as a garnish or side.

Snacks & Street Food

Tostadas

Crispy fried tortillas topped with beans, meat, lettuce, salsa, and cheese. A common antojito (snack).

Quesadillas

Grilled tortillas filled with cheese and sometimes mushrooms, squash blossoms, or meat. Served with salsa or guacamole.

Chicharrón

Fried pork rinds, eaten as a snack or added to dishes for crunch and flavor.

Gorditas

Thick corn cakes split and stuffed with beans, cheese, or meat fillings. Grilled or fried.

Desserts & Sweets

Churros

Fried dough pastries rolled in cinnamon sugar. Often served with chocolate or caramel sauce.

Flan

Rich caramel custard dessert with a silky texture. A staple in Mexican households and restaurants.

Pan de Muerto

Sweet bread decorated with bone-shaped pieces, traditionally eaten during Day of the Dead celebrations.

Drinks

Horchata

Sweet rice milk flavored with cinnamon and vanilla. Refreshing and creamy.

Agua de Jamaica

Hibiscus flower tea served cold. Tart and vibrant in flavor, often sweetened.

Champurrado

Thick chocolate drink made with masa, milk, and spices. Commonly served warm with tamales.
