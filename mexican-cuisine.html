Mexican Cuisine Guide

Rice & Corn Dishes

Arroz Rojo
Mexican Red Rice

Arroz Rojo is a classic Mexican rice dish where the rice is first sautéed in oil, then simmered with a blend of tomatoes, onions, garlic, and chicken broth until it turns fluffy and red. It is typically served as a side dish with beans and a variety of meat entrees.

Tamales

Tamales are made from masa (corn dough) filled with ingredients such as shredded meats, cheese, or chilies. The filled masa is wrapped in corn husks and steamed until firm. They are traditionally prepared for special celebrations and holidays in Mexican households.

Elote

Elote is a popular Mexican street snack consisting of grilled corn on the cob slathered with mayonnaise, cotija cheese, chili powder, and lime juice. It’s a flavorful blend of creamy, tangy, and spicy elements, often eaten as a quick roadside treat.

Meat & Protein-Based Dishes

Tacos al Pastor

Tacos al Pastor are made with pork marinated in achiote, pineapple, and spices, cooked on a vertical spit similar to shawarma. The meat is shaved off and served in soft corn tortillas with chopped onions, cilantro, and salsa for a sweet and savory bite.

Carnitas

Carnitas are slow-cooked pork, typically simmered in lard until tender and then crisped up before serving. The result is juicy meat with caramelized edges, commonly used in tacos, burritos, and tortas across Mexico.

Carne Asada

Carne Asada is grilled beef, often flank or skirt steak, marinated in citrus juice, garlic, and herbs before being cooked over an open flame. Thinly sliced, it's commonly served in tacos or plated with rice and beans.

Chiles Rellenos

Chiles Rellenos are roasted poblano peppers stuffed with cheese or ground meat, coated in egg batter, and fried. They are often served with a savory tomato-based sauce and are a staple dish in Mexican cuisine.

Soups & Stews

Pozole

Pozole is a traditional Mexican soup made from hominy (dried corn kernels), pork or chicken, and a red or green chili broth. It’s garnished with lettuce, radish, onion, lime, and chili flakes and is commonly served during festive gatherings and holidays.

Menudo

Menudo is a spicy soup made from beef tripe and hominy in a red chili base. It is slow-cooked for hours and traditionally enjoyed on weekends or during celebrations. It is also considered a remedy for hangovers in Mexican culture.

Caldo de Pollo

Caldo de Pollo is a comforting Mexican chicken soup featuring chicken pieces simmered with vegetables like carrots, potatoes, and zucchini. It is often served with rice, lime, and warm tortillas, making it a popular family meal.

Snacks & Street Food

Tostadas

Tostadas are crispy, fried or baked corn tortillas topped with layers of refried beans, meat, lettuce, cheese, salsa, and other garnishes. They are crunchy and flavorful, commonly enjoyed as a midday snack or light meal.

Quesadillas

Quesadillas are tortillas filled with cheese and optional ingredients such as mushrooms, squash blossoms, or meat. They are folded and grilled until the cheese melts, then served with guacamole or salsa for dipping.

Chicharrón

Chicharrón is crispy fried pork skin or belly, popular as a snack or topping for dishes like tacos and soups. It delivers a rich, salty flavor and satisfying crunch, often served with lime and hot sauce.
