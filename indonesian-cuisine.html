Indonesian Cuisine Guide

Rice Dishes

Nasi Goreng
Indonesian Fried Rice

Indonesia's famous fried rice, often considered the national dish. Made by stir-frying pre-cooked rice with kecap manis (sweet soy sauce), garlic, shallots, chili, and mixed with vegetables, egg, and sometimes chicken or shrimp. Usually topped with a fried egg and served with crackers (krupuk).

Nasi Uduk

Aromatic rice cooked in coconut milk with lemongrass, bay leaves, and pandan leaves. Typically served with fried chicken, tempeh, boiled egg, fried shallots, and sambal (spicy chili sauce). Popular as breakfast or festive dish in Jakarta.

Nasi Padang

Named after Padang city in West Sumatra, this meal features white rice served with an array of richly spiced side dishes like rendang, sambal, curried vegetables, and fried anchovies. Served buffet-style or in individual portions, known for its bold, spicy flavors.

Nasi Kuning

Yellow rice colored with turmeric and cooked with coconut milk. Traditionally served during celebrations or ceremonies. Often paired with fried chicken, boiled eggs, tempeh, and sambal.

Nasi Campur
Mixed Rice

Literally "mixed rice," it's a plate of white rice surrounded by a variety of meats, vegetables, peanuts, eggs, and crackers. Different regions have their own versions, making it a versatile dish.

Meat & Protein-Based Dishes

Rendang

A slow-cooked dry beef curry originating from the Minangkabau ethnic group. Beef is simmered for hours in coconut milk, chili, garlic, ginger, turmeric, and other spices until tender and deeply flavored. Recognized globally as one of the world's best dishes.

Ayam Goreng
Indonesian Fried Chicken

Indonesian-style fried chicken marinated in garlic, turmeric, coriander, and other spices, then deep-fried to golden perfection. Crispy on the outside, juicy inside. Often eaten with rice and sambal.

Ayam Bakar
Grilled Chicken

Grilled chicken marinated in a mixture of sweet soy sauce, garlic, and spices. Served with rice and sambal, it has a smoky, caramelized flavor.

Sate (Satay)

Skewered and grilled meat, usually chicken, beef, or lamb, marinated in a spiced sauce. Served with a rich, sweet, and slightly spicy peanut sauce. Commonly eaten as street food or at special occasions.

Bakso

Indonesian meatball soup made from beef, chicken, or fish meatballs. Served in a clear broth with noodles, tofu, boiled eggs, and vegetables. Very popular as a quick meal or street food.

Soups & Stews

Soto Ayam

Chicken soup with turmeric-infused broth, served with vermicelli, shredded chicken, boiled eggs, and fried shallots. Served with sambal and lime on the side for added flavor.

Rawon

Beef soup with a deep black broth, flavored with keluak nuts which give it a unique earthy taste. Served with rice, bean sprouts, boiled eggs, and sambal.

Sayur Asem

A sour tamarind-based vegetable soup with peanuts, corn, long beans, and melinjo leaves. Refreshing and light, often eaten with rice.

Gulai

Indonesian curry stew with coconut milk base. Can be made with beef, chicken, fish, or vegetables. Spiced with turmeric, coriander, and chili.

Vegetable Dishes

Gado-Gado

A traditional Indonesian salad with boiled vegetables (potatoes, bean sprouts, spinach), boiled eggs, tofu, and tempeh. Topped with a creamy peanut sauce dressing.

Urap

Steamed vegetables mixed with seasoned grated coconut. Commonly eaten as a side dish.

Pecel

Similar to Gado-Gado but with a thinner peanut sauce. Popular in Java, especially Central Java.

Snacks & Street Food

Tempeh Goreng

Fermented soybean cake deep-fried until crispy. High in protein and commonly eaten as a snack or side dish.

Tahu Isi

Fried tofu stuffed with vegetables or minced meat.

Lemper

Glutinous rice filled with seasoned shredded chicken or beef, wrapped in banana leaves. Often eaten as a snack or on the go.

Martabak Manis

Thick sweet pancake filled with chocolate, cheese, peanuts, and condensed milk. A popular street food dessert.

Martabak Telur

Savory stuffed pancake filled with minced meat, eggs, and scallions.

Desserts & Sweets

Klepon

Sweet glutinous rice balls filled with liquid palm sugar and coated with shredded coconut. Chewy with a burst of sweetness.

Dadar Gulung

Green pandan-flavored crepes rolled with sweet coconut and palm sugar filling.

Es Campur

Shaved ice dessert mixed with various fruits, jelly, sweetened condensed milk, and syrup.

Es Cendol / Dawet

Iced dessert with green rice flour jelly, coconut milk, and palm sugar syrup.

Drinks

Teh Botol

Sweetened bottled jasmine tea, very popular across Indonesia.

Es Teler

Mixed fruit dessert drink with avocado, coconut, jackfruit, sweetened condensed milk, and ice.

Bajigur

Warm traditional drink made from coconut milk, palm sugar, ginger, and pandan leaves, enjoyed in cooler weather.
