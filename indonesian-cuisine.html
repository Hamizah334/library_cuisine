Indonesian Cuisine Guide

Rice Dishes

Nasi Goreng
Indonesian Fried Rice

Nasi Goreng is Indonesia's famous fried rice and often considered the national dish. It is made by stir-frying pre-cooked rice with ingredients such as kecap manis (a thick, sweet soy sauce), garlic, shallots, and chili. Vegetables, egg, and sometimes chicken or shrimp are added to the mixture. It is typically topped with a fried egg and served with crunchy crackers called krupuk, making it a popular and satisfying meal at any time of day.

Nasi Uduk

Nasi Uduk is a fragrant and rich rice dish cooked in coconut milk with aromatic spices like lemongrass, bay leaves, and pandan leaves. Commonly enjoyed in Jakarta, it is often served with a variety of side dishes such as fried chicken, tempeh, boiled egg, fried shallots, and spicy sambal. Its creamy texture and delightful aroma make it a favorite for breakfast and festive occasions.

Nasi Padang

Nasi Padang refers to a style of serving rice from Padang, West Sumatra, known for its bold and spicy flavors. White rice is paired with a variety of side dishes, including beef rendang, sambal, spicy egg curries, fried anchovies, and vegetables in coconut milk. It can be served in a buffet style or in individual portions, and is renowned across Indonesia for its complexity and richness.

Nasi Kuning

Nasi Kuning, or yellow rice, gets its vibrant color from turmeric and its rich flavor from coconut milk. Traditionally served during special occasions and celebrations, this dish is commonly accompanied by fried chicken, boiled eggs, sambal, and fried tempeh. Its symbolic color represents prosperity and good fortune in Indonesian culture.

Rendang

Rendang is a legendary Indonesian dry beef curry originating from the Minangkabau ethnic group. It is prepared by slowly cooking beef in a mixture of coconut milk, chili, garlic, ginger, galangal, turmeric, and other spices until the liquid evaporates and the meat becomes tender and deeply flavorful. Frequently listed as one of the world’s best dishes, Rendang is a festive dish often reserved for special occasions.

Ayam Goreng
Indonesian Fried Chicken

Ayam Goreng is Indonesia’s take on fried chicken. The chicken is marinated in a blend of garlic, coriander, turmeric, and other local spices before being deep-fried to golden perfection. It has a crispy exterior while maintaining a juicy interior, and is often served with rice and sambal for a spicy kick. This dish is a popular choice in home cooking and warung eateries across Indonesia.

Ayam Bakar
Grilled Chicken

Ayam Bakar is Indonesian grilled chicken, marinated in a mixture of sweet soy sauce, garlic, shallots, and aromatic spices. After marinating, the chicken is grilled over charcoal until it’s smoky and caramelized. It is commonly served with steamed rice and sambal, delivering a perfect blend of sweet, savory, and spicy flavors in every bite.

Sate (Satay)

Sate, or Satay, is a popular Indonesian street food made of skewered and grilled meat — typically chicken, beef, or lamb. The meat is marinated in a flavorful mixture of spices and herbs, then grilled and served with a thick, sweet, and slightly spicy peanut sauce. It’s a festive dish found at markets, food stalls, and family celebrations throughout the archipelago.

Bakso

Bakso is Indonesia’s beloved meatball soup, typically made from finely ground beef, although variations using chicken or fish are also common. The meatballs are served in a clear broth alongside noodles, tofu, hard-boiled eggs, and vegetables. It is a comfort food and street food staple, enjoyed by people of all ages throughout the country.

Soups & Stews

Soto Ayam

Soto Ayam is a traditional Indonesian chicken soup with a turmeric-infused broth that gives it a vibrant yellow color. It is typically served with vermicelli noodles, shredded chicken, boiled eggs, fried shallots, and a squeeze of lime. Accompanied by sambal, this warming soup is a favorite across the islands for breakfast or lunch.

Rawon

Rawon is a unique and hearty beef soup known for its dark broth, which comes from the use of keluak (black nuts). This traditional East Javanese dish features tender beef chunks in an earthy, nutty broth, often served with rice, bean sprouts, salted egg, and sambal. Its deep flavor makes it stand out among Indonesian soups.

Vegetable Dishes

Gado-Gado

Gado-Gado is a beloved Indonesian salad consisting of boiled vegetables such as spinach, bean sprouts, potatoes, and cabbage, served with tofu, tempeh, and hard-boiled eggs. It is generously topped with a rich peanut sauce dressing and fried shallots. Gado-Gado is both nutritious and filling, offering a vegetarian-friendly option in Indonesian cuisine.
